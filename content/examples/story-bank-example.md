# Story Bank — Master STAR+R Stories

This file accumulates your best interview stories over time. Each evaluation's Interview Prep section adds new stories here. Instead of memorizing 100 answers, maintain 5-10 deep stories that you can bend to answer almost any behavioral question.

## How it works

1. Every time `/sur9e offer` generates Interview Prep, new STAR+R stories get appended here.
2. Before your next interview, review this file — your stories are already organized by theme.
3. The "Big Three" questions can be answered with stories from this bank:
   - "Tell me about yourself" → combine 2-3 stories into a narrative
   - "Tell me about your most impactful project" → pick your highest-impact story
   - "Tell me about a conflict you resolved" → find a story with a Reflection

## Format (STAR+R)

Every story uses five sections:

- **S (Situation)** — context, scope, constraints
- **T (Task)** — what specifically had to be done
- **A (Action)** — what _you_ did (use "I", not "we", and name specific decisions/tools)
- **R (Result)** — measurable outcome (numbers > adjectives)
- **R (Reflection)** — what you learned, what you'd do differently

Tag each story with themes in the heading: `[AI / Production]`, `[Leadership]`, `[Conflict]`, `[Migration]`, etc. Multiple themes per story are fine.

### [Leadership / Hiring] Example: Building the engineering team from 2 → 9

**Source:** Acme Corp — Engineering Lead (Jan 2024) · [acme.example](https://acme.example)
**S (Situation):** Acme had hit product-market fit but the eng team was 2 people. Six months to either scale or get acquired below valuation.
**T (Task):** Hire and onboard 7 engineers without breaking velocity on the core product.
**A (Action):** Wrote the interview rubric from scratch (3 stages, calibrated weekly with the CTO). Sourced through targeted Slack communities and 2 referral cycles instead of inbound. Pair-programmed with each new hire on day 1 to set the bar. Established a weekly architecture review so context didn't bottleneck through me.
**R (Result):** 7 hires in 5 months, 0 regrettable attrition in 12 months, velocity tripled measured in shipped features per sprint.
**R (Reflection):** Calibrating the rubric weekly (vs. a one-time spec) was the highest-leverage decision. Mistake: I underestimated onboarding cost — the first 2 hires would have ramped 3 weeks faster if I'd written the runbook before they started.
